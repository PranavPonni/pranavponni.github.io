Overview#

TaSA (Two‑Phased deep learning of Tactile Sensory Attenuation) instantiates the neuroscience idea of sensory attenuation on robotic fingertips. The key idea is to predict the tactile signal caused by the robot itself (self‑touch) and subtract it from the total tactile reading at run time, leaving a cleaner external contact signal for control.

Hardware: Allegro Hand + XELA uSkin fingertips. Tasks: precision insertions (e.g., mechanical pencil lead), where finger–finger and finger–palm contacts otherwise confound detection of true object contact.

Framework#

  • Phase 1 — Self‑Touch Prediction: learn a forward model that maps joint motion (and current tactile) to the next‑step tactile field.
  • Phase 2 — Motion Learning: use external = total − self as input to a policy that generates joint commands for in‑hand manipulation; self‑contact is down‑weighted while unexpected external contact is emphasized.

Mathematical Formulation#

Self‑touch predictor:

$$\hat{\mathbf T}_{t+1} = f_{\theta}(\mathbf J_t,\, \mathbf J_{t+1},\, \mathbf T_t)$$

Training loss:

$$\mathcal L_{\text{self}} = \lVert \mathbf T_{t+1} - \hat{\mathbf T}_{t+1} \rVert_1$$

External tactile (sensory attenuation):

$$\mathbf T^{\text{ext}}_t = \mathbf T_t - \hat{\mathbf T}_t$$

Motion policy:

$$\mathbf a_t = g_{\phi}(\mathbf T^{\text{ext}}_t,\, \mathbf T_t,\, \mathbf J_t)$$

with task‑level objective (e.g., insertion success) and regularizers that discourage force spikes due to self‑contact.
